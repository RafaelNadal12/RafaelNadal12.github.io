All About Animals

Hello. On this website I will teach you all about different animals.


These are the top 10 most dangerous animals:

  1. Grizzly Bear: Because of its great size and strength the grizzly bear is considered to be one of the most dangerous animals in the world. Although it doesn't usually come in contact with humans, it is still a danger to anything it sees as a threat.



  2. Great White Shark: The Great White Shark, notorious for it's appearence in the movie Jaws, is one of the most dangerous animals on Earth. It's speed added on to it's massive size makes it an apex predator in the ocean. Although Great White Sharks rarely encounter humans they can still very dangerous.



  3. Hippopotamus: Although the hippo may look docile at first glance, it is quite the opposite. It is one of the animals with the most human victims in all of Africa. Despite it's unathletic nature, the hippo can run at 19 miles per hour.



  4. Stonefish: The Stonefish, like its name, is shaped like a stone or piece of coral. Because of it's tendency to live near the shore, it is often stepped on by people to which it injects it's lethal poison causing unbearable pain.



  5. African Buffalo: The African Buffalo is known by several hunters as "The Black Death." It's ability to run at 40 miles per hour coupled with it's great size makes it one of the most dangerous animals in the world. It is estimated that it is responsible for 200 deaths annualy just in the African continent.



  6. Lion: The king of the jungle cannot stay out of this list. Being one of the largest cats in the world it is no surprise that they are a danger to humans. Annualy, there are about 500 attacks on humans. It is also believed that after a lion succesfully hunts a human, it will try again.



  7. Saltwater Crocodile: A speed of 18 miles per hour and having perhaps the strongest jaw in the animal kingdom makes the saltwater crocodile one of the most dangerous animals in the world. This species ways up to 2600 pounds and up to 20 feet long. The saltwater crocodile is the largest reptile in the world. Unlike other animals on this list, the saltwater crocodile sees humans as prey so it is best to stay away from this creature.



  8. Black Mamba: The black mamba does not attack humans unless provoked. It is known as the most poisonus snake in all of Africa. This snake injects 100 grams of poison, 10 times the amount needed to kill a human. It is 10 feet long and can attack prey up to 40 yards away!



  9. Box Jellyfish: This jellyfish's tentacles can reach up to an astounding 10 feet! Altough not an agressive animal, the box jellyfish has the ability to kill a human in 5 minutes! The pain can be described as being touched by hot lava.



  10. Mosquito: Although this animal does not directly attack humans, it is still an incredible danger as this animal can carry hundreds of diseases and parasites that can eventually lead to death. An incredible 725,000 people die due to diseases spread by mosquitos. Although not as fearsome as the other animals on this list, mosquitos are in fact, the most dangerous animal in the world.



Honorable Mentions Include: African Elephants, Humans, Komodo Dragons.


These are the top 10 weirdest animals:

  1. Blue-Footed Booby: As the name suggests, the Blue-Footed Booby is the only animal with bright blue feet. During mating season the Blue-Footed Booby lifts its feet up and down to attract mates.



  2. Japanese Spider Crab: This species of crabs clumber over one another creating great mounds nearly 100 meters long! They also have the largest legs of any arthropod on Earth.



  3. Sparkle Muffin: It's elaborate shape and vibrant colors resemble a tiny peacock's tail. Male sparkle muffin's do a little dance to impress females.



  4. Mantis Shrimp: Both eyes of the mantis shrimp work independently to better target its prey. It's eyes can detect more colors than any other animal on Earth!



  5. Axolotl: The axolotl has the highest regenerative ability of any four-footed animal. This means that similar to starfish the axolotl can regrow limbs!



  6. Saiga Antelope: If you look closely at the saiga antelope you would see a gigantic nose. During the summer, the snout helps to cool of the saiga's blood. In the winter it is used to heat up the air before it reaches the lungs.



  7. Dumbo Octopus: The dumbo octopus is named after the elephant dumbo in the infamous disney movie. It has 2 fins that eerily resemble wings. This species is one of the deepest living octopuses in the world. Unlike most other octopuses the dumbo octopus does not produce ink.



  8. Aye-Aye: Similar to rodents, the aye-aye has teeth that never stop growing. It is said that if an aye-aye looks at you or points at you it is bad luck. In order to find food, the aye-aye taps on trees to find hollow chambers. Then, it chews holes with its teeth and pulls grubs with it's skinny little fingers.



  9. Platypus: The platypus is a duck-billed, beaver-tailed, otter-footed, egg-laying aquatic creature native to Australia. It has webbed feet that make it a strong swimmer. The platypus is the only member of the Ornithorhynchidae family. The platypus is the only animal that is part mammal, reptile, and bird.



  10. Blobfish: This fish is objectively the ugliest animal in the universe. It has almost no muscles or bones. It's extremely high fat content allows it to go into deep waters that other fish cannot survive in. It is, without question, the weirdest animal on Earth.




Honorable Mentions: Hagfish, Chinese Water Deer, Vampire Bat, Lyrebird, Pacu Fish
